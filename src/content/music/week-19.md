Week 3 built the major sentence; the minor key has been waiting sixteen weeks for its
own. **iiø7 – V7♭9 – i** — the half-diminished chord with its sunken fifth, the dominant
pressing a raw flat nine, the tonic arriving with its ache intact — is the harmonic
spine of Autumn Leaves, My Funny Valentine, and every torch song that ever made a room
go quiet. It also keeps week 15's promise: the shivering diminished chord was a rootless
flat-nine all along. Minor's grammar, completed. Press gently.
