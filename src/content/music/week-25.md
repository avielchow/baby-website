Twenty-four weeks on the sharp side and home keys; this week the course crosses the
circle. **F major** — one flat, warmer light, the neighborhood where nocturnes, jazz
standards, and Una Mattina all keep house — hosts the axis progression's third and
final tour. New geography under the hands (B♭ beneath the ring finger, C major suddenly
working as someone else's dominant), Einaudi's broken-octave engine in the left hand,
and the discovery that keys aren't transpositions of each other so much as different
rooms with different acoustics. The velvet side. You'll be back often.
