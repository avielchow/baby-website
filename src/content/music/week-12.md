Every week until now ended where it began. This week, the door: **modulation** — the
pivot chord with two passports, the V-of-V that lifts, the suspended old tonic waving
from the border, and a G major chord that decides to stop being a dominant and start
being an address. It's the biggest structural move in tonal music and Whitney's final
chorus made it a national event. Yours can be quieter: somewhere in bar four, over a
sleeping boy, a key change nobody notices — except that the room feels one step
brighter, and you did that with three fingers and a plan.
