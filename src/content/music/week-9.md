Eight weeks of major keys with minor visitors; this week the house changes hands.
**A minor, on the lament bass** — the four-hundred-year-old staircase of A–G–F–E —
with the week's quiet miracle sitting in plain sight: your right hand holds ONE shape
for three bars while the falling floor recomposes it, and then a single borrowed G♯
lights the candle that leads everything home. The saddest music in the world is mostly
this: stillness upstairs, gravity downstairs, and one note that refuses to give up.
