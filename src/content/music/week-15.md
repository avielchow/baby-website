Season two opens on the crack in the sidewalk. **The passing diminished** — one black-key
bass note wearing a shivering little chord — is how stride pianists, gospel players, and
standards writers make plain progressions saunter instead of walk. Fourteen weeks built
the rooms of the house; this season is about the doorways, and this is the prettiest one:
four notes, no fixed root, every voice leaning half a step toward home. Fats Waller made
a career of that lean. One bar a week of it will change your hands.
