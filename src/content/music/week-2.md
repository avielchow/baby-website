Week two takes the most common progression in pop — vi–IV–I–V, the one under a thousand
songs — and refuses to play it plainly. Every chord gets one extra ringing note: a 9, a
major 7th, a suspension. Your fingers already reach these notes by accident; this week
names them, and the six-note wave in the left hand turns the whole thing into water. By
the end you'll have a theme of your own built from a three-note cell — which is exactly
how "River Flows in You" was built, and it flows just as well over a crib rail at 10pm.
