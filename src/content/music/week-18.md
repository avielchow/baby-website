Season one taught the 9 as a guest — one shimmer per progression. This week it moves in.
**Four ninth chords in a row** — maj9, m9, m9, dominant 9 — and the discovery that holds
them together: every ninth chord is a friendly old seventh chord standing on a deeper
root, and every voicing follows one rule (root low, 3-and-7 in the middle, color on top).
This is the full Yiruma texture, the reason his two-chord bars sound like weather. Wide
cushion, four glowing stacks, a melody that lands only on the money notes. Rain, on
purpose, all week.
