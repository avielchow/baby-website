Nine weeks of bass lines walking, falling, and leading — and now the counter-lesson:
**what happens when the bass refuses to move at all.** A pedal point turns harmony
inside out: the chords stop being places you go and become weather passing over one
fixed field. Add an ostinato on top — three notes, repeated, recolored — and you've
built the machine behind Clocks, most film scores, and every piece of music designed
for the specific magic of making a small person's eyelids heavy. Stillness, it turns
out, is a technique.
