Fourteen weeks ago this was a plan: one progression at a time, for a classically-trained
pair of hands that never got the names. This week it's a single eight-bar sentence that
uses almost all of it — the descending line grown longer, the shimmer, the borrowed rain,
the held breath, the turnaround — under a melody that isn't improvised anymore, because
you're going to keep it. **Write the theme. Write it down. Play it the same way twice.**
Somewhere down the hall is the person it's for, currently the size of a loaf of bread,
who will never remember a time before it existed. That's what a family song is. Welcome
to the end of the course and the beginning of the repertoire.
