Week 4 taught the rain; week 5 taught the sun after it; nineteen weeks later, the secret:
**they were two halves of one cadence all along.** The backdoor — iv7 to ♭VII9, sliding
up a whole step into a glowing tonic — is jazz's gentlest way home: no leading tone, no
tritone press, just two borrowed flats letting go at once like weather clearing. It's
the door The Christmas Song opens with, the sound of Nat King Cole deciding a room
should feel warmer. Front doors are for arriving. This one is for coming home.
