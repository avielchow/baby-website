Seven weeks in one key was a choice: every trick had to earn its name before it moved
house. This week it moves. **Week 1's progression, transposed to G major** — same
numerals, same thread-note trick, same descending bass — and the discovery that your
hands already know it, because what they learned was never 'C major facts.' It was
grammar. Play it side by side with week 1 and listen to the piano itself brighten;
then take it to a third key, badly, on purpose. Twelve doors, one key in your pocket.
