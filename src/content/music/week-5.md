Last week you borrowed rain; this week, from the same neighbor, **sunlight**. The ♭VII
is the other face of modal mixture — the chord that erases urgency instead of adding
sorrow, the sound of the Hey Jude coda circling forever because nothing needs resolving
anymore. Faster tempo, dry pedal, five-note melodies you could sing with a mouthful of
toast. After a month of beautiful ache, consider this week the palate cleanser: music
that has nowhere to be and is delighted about it.
