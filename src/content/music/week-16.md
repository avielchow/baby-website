Week 11's falling line has a twin that climbs. **The ascending cliché** — G creeping up
through G♯ and A to B♭ while everything around it holds still — is the sound of hope
assembling itself, and its vehicle is the augmented chord: rootless, weightless, all
anticipation. Michelle runs on it. Half the great torch songs end their bridges with it.
And it hands you a genuinely pianistic skill: voicing one singing line inside a quiet
hand — three voices, ten fingers, one story climbing the stairs while the house holds
its breath.
