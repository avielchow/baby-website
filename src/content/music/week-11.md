Ten weeks of chords chosen one by one; this week a single falling voice chooses them
for you. **The line cliché** — C sliding to A♭ one candle-step at a time — is the
songwriter's oldest sleight of hand: it sounds like sophisticated harmony (maj7! secondary
dominant! borrowed iv!) but it's really just one finger walking downstairs while the
house holds still. Something opens with it. Michelle leans on it. And now, over one
particular bassinet in Alberta, so do you.
