Six weeks of pop ballads; tonight, the lounge. **The circle of fifths** is what harmony
does when nobody interferes — roots falling a 5th at a time, each chord handing two of
its notes to the next like a relay — and iii–vi–ii–V is the songbook's classic long way
home. The revelation this week isn't a color note; it's that the right two notes (the
guide tones) make four chords sound like an arrangement. Shells in the left hand, a
five-note ladder in the right, and suddenly you're the piano player at the wedding.
Somebody fly this baby to the moon.
