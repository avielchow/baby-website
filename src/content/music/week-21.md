Weeks 9 and 19 taught minor as longing — candles, flat nines, resolution earned. This
week, an older answer. **Dorian** raises one note of the minor scale and cancels the
grief machinery entirely: a warm IV where the sad iv should be, loops that walk in
circles instead of arguing home, melodies that carried themselves across five centuries
without a single accompanying chord. It's the mode of Scarborough Fair and of every
tune that sounds like it was always there. Hold an open 5th, raise the 6th, and walk.
