Cody finally came home on Monday, July 20th. The drive was terrifying — such a tiny boy in the back seat — and it was also where we saw his first smile. He was delighted every time we accelerated. Noted, son.

We got home to the cats conducting a full investigation. We set him down in the crib, and I was surprised how strong his grip is — he wraps all his little fingers around my index finger and will not let go.

We learned so much ahead of time, and there were still so many surprises. The biggest one, for me, was how hard Mom's recovery really is. Jenn couldn't do anything at first — even standing up took effort, and sitting down hurt a lot. And even with everything hurting, she still carried the emotional weight of a (temporarily) multigenerational home, of motherhood, of feeding expectations, of hormones, all at once. This was the week I really learned that all moms are supermoms. Go Mom.

The second-biggest surprise was cluster feeding — this boy is *hungry*. I started to recognize the feeding cues and realized most of his crying was just him ordering more milk. On the plus side: diaper changes are surprisingly easy so far. On day three he peed all over the change pad mid-change and sprayed the wall, and honestly it was pretty funny.

The nights are by far the hardest. From Friday to Wednesday I got maybe one or two broken-up hours of sleep a day, and I was starting to get really grouchy. Then Wednesday evening the grandparents took over, Cody staged a no-pee, no-poo evening, and I slept five consecutive hours. Wednesday night was a game changer for another reason too: we decided not to breastfeed at night, which meant I could do everything quickly myself — and even collect three broken one-hour blocks of sleep.

Wednesday was also the day the Mom v. Mom conflict peaked. Highlights: grandma wanted to throw away Jenn's first breast milk (bottles being, apparently, bad), and since Mom can only eat boiled fruit, Jenn's favorite white peaches were all boiled. All of them. But we had a family talk, and Thursday was much better. Thursday, July 23rd, has actually been the best day so far — we even had time to film a few precious moments.

So, Cody: I wonder if we're right about your personality. So far, we think you love eating and sleeping more than anything else. You came out big and you are always hungry. Your mom only ever craved burgers while you were in her belly, so I got to eat burgers because of you — I wonder if that will hold. You have a strong grip and you're easily satisfied. We think you'll be carefree and easy-going, which is why we loved the name Cody. We hope you'll be the magic combination: kind, easy-going, carefree, happy, curious, and intellectual.

One week down. You're doing great. Your parents are doing their best.
