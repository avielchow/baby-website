It's hour five in a triage room, and I'm writing this from the chair beside Jenn's bed. We were supposed to be induced on July 10th. It is now July 17th. We have been on hold for a week, which means we have celebrated our last day of freedom approximately seven times.

(A note for the record, since this journal will mostly be me doing the typing: this is Dad writing. Jenn has the considerably harder job right now.)

Before all this, we stocked up like we were preparing for a siege — fruit, snacks, an optimistic number of watermelons, all meant for when we came home with a baby. We have almost eaten all of it. Baby, apparently, is just too happy in there overcooking.

The week itself was two things at once. Anxious limbo: every phone ring might be the hospital, and Jenn had her bouts of impatience as expected calls didn't come. But mostly, once we accepted we were on hold, it turned into a weird bonus vacation — found time we never planned on having.

So here's what we did with it. I discovered cortados, which I now really like. On what we thought was our actual last day, Jenn made me a great one with Manner coffee from China, and we had some amazing latte art at Aggudo in Kensington. We put the final touches on the nursery — added lamps, made a slightly frantic Ikea run for more shelving, and brought home an olive tree that turned out to be the perfect final touch against the bookshelves. The decal, the olive tree, and the mint green Ikea photo frames are our favorites. We practiced filming and made two proudly amateur videos. And this entire website — the journal you're reading right now — was built in the last two days, which tells you exactly what my version of nesting looks like.

We're so grateful for all the love this week — everyone checking in, asking if he's here yet. Not yet. This morning the phone finally rang for real, so here we are: hour five of triage, hospital bags at our feet.

Hopefully we get into a labor and delivery room today. The triage bed is comfy, at least. The next time I write here, everything will be different.
