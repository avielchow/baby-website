weekNumber: 1
title: "The first days"
description: "The first week is about surviving the shock, keeping baby fed, and learning to read his cues. Almost nothing has a schedule yet."
milestones:
  - title: "Reflexes present"
    detail: "Rooting, sucking, grasp, Moro (startle), and stepping reflexes are all present at birth and are what the pediatrician checks."
  - title: "Vision at about 8–12 inches"
    detail: "That's about the distance from your face when you're holding him. He'll focus on faces above objects."
  - title: "Regains birth weight by ~day 10–14"
    detail: "It's normal to lose up to 7% of birth weight in the first few days and regain it by two weeks."
todos:
  - title: "First pediatrician visit within 3–5 days of discharge"
    detail: "Weight check, jaundice screen, feeding review. Book this before you leave the hospital if you can."
  - title: "Register the birth"
    detail: "In Alberta this is via Vital Statistics; the hospital usually gives you the packet. Do it in the first two weeks."
  - title: "Add baby to your provincial health card and to any private insurance"
    detail: "Most private plans have a 30-day window from birth to add without underwriting."
  - title: "Install and check the car seat before discharge"
    detail: "The hospital may verify it's installed correctly. Rear-facing, tightly anchored, chest clip at armpit level."
feeding:
  - title: "Feed on demand, every 1–3 hours"
    detail: "8–12 feeds in 24 hours is typical for the first weeks, day and night. Cluster feeding in the evening is normal."
  - title: "Watch for at least 6 wet diapers per day by day 5"
    detail: "Fewer than that + fewer than 3–4 stools = call the pediatrician or a lactation consultant."
  - title: "Colostrum first, then milk 'comes in' around day 3–5"
    detail: "Small volumes of colostrum (teaspoons) are enough in the first days. Baby's stomach is walnut-sized."
sleep:
  - title: "16–18 hours of sleep spread across the day and night"
    detail: "No circadian rhythm yet — expect no distinction between day and night."
  - title: "Back to sleep, on a firm flat surface, nothing loose in the crib"
    detail: "AAP + Canadian Paediatric Society safe-sleep guidance. No pillows, bumpers, blankets, or stuffed toys until 12 months."
  - title: "Room-share (not bed-share) for the first 6 months"
    detail: "Reduces SIDS risk. Bassinet or crib in your room."
redFlags:
  - title: "Fever (rectal temp ≥ 38.0°C / 100.4°F)"
    detail: "Any fever in a baby under 3 months is a same-day medical assessment. No exceptions."
  - title: "Yellow skin or eyes worsening after day 5"
    detail: "Some jaundice is normal; worsening jaundice, or jaundice with poor feeding/sleepiness, warrants a call."
  - title: "Won't wake to feed, or feeds < every 4 hours"
    detail: "Newborns should not be sleeping through feeds this early."
  - title: "Fewer than 6 wet diapers by day 5"
    detail: "Sign of inadequate intake — call the pediatrician or a lactation consultant."
  - title: "Persistent vomiting (not spit-up), especially green or bloody"
    detail: "Spit-up is common. True projectile vomiting or any green/bloody vomit is an emergency."
alsoWorthKnowing:
  - title: "The first-week baby blues are extremely common"
    detail: "Hormonal, sleep-deprived tears in the first 1–2 weeks affect ~70–80% of birthing parents. If it persists past 2 weeks or includes hopelessness or intrusive thoughts, that's postpartum depression territory — call your provider."
  - title: "Skin-to-skin is genuinely regulating for both of you"
    detail: "Stabilises baby's temperature, heart rate, and blood sugar; supports feeding and bonding. Do it as much as you want."
